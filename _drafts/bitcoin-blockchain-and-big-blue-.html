Last February (has it been that long ago already?) the Wallstreet Journal posted that

IBM is becoming the biggest backer of blockchain technology, and taking a leading role in the development of innovative, next generation blockchain applications beyond Bitcoin.

This quote was part of my recent presentation at the Guide Executive Council in Madrid, where I presented the blockchain concept (and the bitcoin implementation) to the attendees.

After giving the presentation and hearing the remarks (mostly positive I can assure you) of the audience the concept of blockchain in relation to The Mainframe really started sinking in to me.

As we all know The Mainframe can be equipped with a Crypto Express 2 card. A card well equipped for performing Elliptic Curve Cryptography as well as various hashing algorithms. And this got me thinking. It got me thinking a lot to be honest.....

What if we would join (or start) a mining pool on z13's, Rockhoppers and Emperors?

We would call it the zPool and have a bunch of Mainframes from all over the world join in. I truly believe that a couple of these machines would totally outrun setups like these. They would most definitely be more quiet :)
